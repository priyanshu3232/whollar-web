Privacy Policy

Effective [date] · Last updated [date]

This Privacy Policy explains how Whollar [Inc./Ltd.] (“Whollar,” “we,” “us”) collects, uses, discloses, and protects personal information when you use whollar.ca, providers.whollar.ca, and our services (the “Service”). We handle personal information in line with Canada’s Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA), Quebec’s Law 25, and Canada’s Anti-Spam Legislation (CASL).

The short version

Privacy at a glance

  • Whollar is free for you, and we don’t sell your personal information.
  • We collect what we need to check if you’re overpaying, group you into a cohort, and help you switch — if you choose to.
  • You’re in control: you can access your information, correct it, withdraw consent, and ask us to delete it.
  • We use automated tools to read the bills you upload; you always decide whether to switch.

The sections below explain each of these in full.

2

Who’s responsible for your information

Whollar is responsible for the personal information in our care. Our Privacy Officer handles questions and requests — contact [privacy@whollar.ca].

3

What we collect

We collect only what we need to run the Service:

  • Identity & contact: your name, email address, and (if you provide it) phone number.
  • Location & serviceability: your service address and/or postal code, so we can tell which Providers and offers are available to you and place you in the right cohort.
  • Your internet bill: the bill you upload and the details we extract from it — such as your current provider, plan name, speed, monthly cost, contract or promotional end dates, and the service address on the bill.
  • Usage & device: how you use the Service, collected through cookies and analytics (see Cookies).
What we don’t collect: We don’t need or ask for your Social Insurance Number, or your banking or account passwords, to use the core Service. Please don’t send these to us.
4

How we use your information

We use your information to:

  • assess whether you may be overpaying and estimate potential savings;
  • group your household into a regional cohort and run reverse-bid auctions with Providers;
  • present offers to you and, if you accept one, help complete your switch;
  • provide support, keep the Service secure, and meet legal obligations; and
  • with your consent, send you information about Whollar and relevant offers.

We rely on your consent, and on the other bases the law permits, to do these things.

5

Automated processing of your bill

When you upload a bill, we use automated extraction (including machine-learning tools) to read it and pull out the details listed above, so you don’t have to type them in. This speeds things up and reduces error. We don’t make decisions that have a legal or similarly significant effect on you by automated means alone — you decide whether to accept any offer and whether to switch. You can ask us about this processing, or ask for a person to review, using the contact details below.

6

Marketing and your CASL consent

We’ll only send you commercial electronic messages (such as promotional emails) if you’ve given consent, and every message includes an easy way to unsubscribe. You can withdraw consent at any time. We’ll still send you messages needed to run a service you’ve asked for — such as auction results or account notices.

7

When we share your information

We don’t sell your personal information. We share it only as follows:

  • Service providers: We use trusted providers — including Zoho (Zoho Catalyst for our backend and Zoho CRM as our system of record) — to host and process information on our behalf, under contracts that require them to protect it and use it only for us.
  • Providers in an auction: To let Providers bid for a cohort, we share cohort information in aggregated or de-identified form wherever possible. We share the personal details a Provider needs to onboard you only after you choose that Provider’s offer.
  • Legal & safety: We may disclose information where required by law, to enforce our Terms, or to protect people’s safety.
  • Business transfers: If Whollar is involved in a merger, financing, or sale, information may be transferred as part of that, subject to this Policy.
8

Where your information is processed

Your information is processed and stored using our service providers’ infrastructure. We aim to keep personal information within Canada. Where information is stored or processed outside your province or outside Canada, it may be subject to the laws of that jurisdiction, and we take steps to ensure it’s protected to a comparable standard.

Before publishing: confirm and state the specific Zoho data-residency region here (e.g. a Canadian data centre). Naming where data is stored — and flagging any transfer outside Quebec/Canada — is a required disclosure under Law 25, and matches the residency verification you’d already flagged as a priority.
9

Consent

By using the Service and providing your information, you consent to the collection, use, and disclosure of your personal information as described in this Policy. Where required, we’ll ask for your express consent (for example, before sending marketing messages). You can withdraw consent at any time, subject to legal or contractual limits — though some withdrawals may mean we can no longer provide part of the Service.

10

Your rights

Depending on where you live, and under PIPEDA and Law 25, you can:

  • access the personal information we hold about you, and ask how it’s been used and shared;
  • ask us to correct information that’s inaccurate or incomplete;
  • withdraw your consent;
  • ask us to delete your information or stop processing it; and
  • receive certain information you provided in a structured, commonly used technological format (data portability).

To make a request, contact our Privacy Officer at [privacy@whollar.ca]. We’ll respond within the timeframes the law requires. If you’re not satisfied, you can contact the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada, or, in Quebec, the Commission d’accès à l’information du Québec.

11

How long we keep your information

We keep personal information only as long as we need it for the purposes in this Policy, or as the law requires, and then we securely delete or anonymize it. You can ask us to delete your information sooner (see Your rights).

12

How we protect your information

We use appropriate physical, technical, and organizational safeguards to protect your information against loss, theft, and unauthorized access, use, or disclosure. Bills you upload are transmitted over a secure connection, and access is limited to what’s needed to run the Service. No system is perfectly secure, but we work to keep your information safe — and to notify you and the relevant authorities if a breach creates a risk of serious harm, as the law requires.

13

Cookies

We use cookies and similar technologies to run the Service, remember your preferences, and understand how the Service is used so we can improve it. Most browsers let you refuse or delete cookies; if you do, some parts of the Service may not work as well. We use analytics data in aggregate to see which pages are useful.

14

Links to other sites

Our Service may link to other websites, including Providers’. We’re not responsible for their privacy practices. Review their policies before sharing information with them.

15

Children

The Service isn’t directed to, or intended for, anyone under the age of majority in their province or territory, and we don’t knowingly collect their information.

16

Changes to this Policy

We may update this Policy from time to time. We’ll post the updated version here and change the “Last updated” date. Significant changes will be communicated where appropriate.
